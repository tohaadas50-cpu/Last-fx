# Lateral Thinking Framework — TextFX Creative Engine

This document outlines the lateral thinking methodology embedded in TextFX's three core modules. The system is designed to think like a seasoned Creative Director: breaking conventions, finding paradoxes, and crafting emotionally honest campaigns.

## Core Philosophy

**Standard Thinking (Linear)**: Product feature → Target audience → Campaign message.  
**Lateral Thinking (Creative)**: Human truth ← Paradox ← Constraint Reversal ← Hidden opportunity.

TextFX reverses the flow: it starts with contradictions (what do people secretly want vs. what they say they want?) and mines those tensions for breakthrough ideas.

---

## Module 1: Insight Generator — Constraint Reversal & Metaphor Mining

### Technique 1: Constraint Reversal
Instead of solving the *stated* problem, invert it and ask: "What if the problem is actually the solution?"

**Example (Electric Kettle)**:
- Stated need: "Boil water fast"
- Reversal: "What if SLOW is the luxury? What if the kettle should make you pause, not rush?"
- Insight: Speed is expected. Pause is precious. The kettle becomes a time object, not a boiling device.

### Technique 2: Unexpected Connections (Cross-Domain Linking)
Link the product to unrelated domains to unlock hidden associations.

**Example**:
- Kettle + Luxury Perfume → Ritual, scarcity, identity
- Kettle + Meditation Practice → Control, mindfulness, presence
- Kettle + Architectural Design → Form, materials, quiet power

These connections reveal deeper customer needs.

### Technique 3: Metaphor Mining
Ask: **"What is the product secretly?"**

Not "what does it do?" but "what is it *really*?"

- A kettle isn't just a heating device.
- It's a **pause button**, a **ritual guardian**, a **permission slip to slow down**.

This shift unlocks emotional positioning instead of functional positioning.

### Technique 4: Tension Mapping
Find the contradictions in the customer's actual behavior.

**Example Tensions**:
- They say "I want fast delivery" but spend time curating slow mornings.
- They say "convenience" but crave ritual and intention.
- They chase productivity but secretly starve for pause.

**The insight**: Solve the *emotional* tension, not the stated one.

---

## Module 2: Concept Mapper — Cognitive Shift & Emotional Paradox

### Technique 1: Cognitive Shift (Category Reframe)
Move the product out of its expected category into a new one.

**Example (Kettle)**:
- Standard category: "Home appliance" → Features: speed, efficiency.
- Shifted category: "Lifestyle object" / "Time curator" → Benefit: intentionality, identity.

This single reframe changes everything. Now the campaign isn't about features; it's about who the person *becomes* when they use it.

### Technique 2: Emotional Subversion (Saying the Quiet Part Loud)
Identify what the customer *really* wants beneath the surface request.

**Example**:
- They ask for: "A fast kettle"
- They feel: "I'm overwhelmed and need control"
- They secretly crave: "Permission to pause without guilt"

A subversive creative concept honors this paradox. Instead of "boils in 2 minutes," say: "Three minutes that matter."

### Technique 3: Metaphor as Strategy
Build the entire concept around a central metaphor.

**Example**:
- "The kettle as a beacon in morning chaos"
- "Three minutes: a ritual anchor"
- "Steam rising: time made visible"

The metaphor becomes the *language* of the entire campaign—copy, visuals, sound, everything speaks this way.

### Technique 4: Emotional Arc (Not Feature Arc)
Define the emotional journey the campaign takes the customer on.

**Example**:
- Start: Rushed, overwhelmed, identity-less
- Middle: Pause, recognition, choice
- End: Grounded, intentional, whole

The product isn't the hero—the *customer's transformation* is.

## Module 3: Script Writer — Emotional Beats & Narrative Subversion

### Technique 1: Emotional Beats (3-Act Micro-Drama)
Structure the script as an emotional arc, not a feature list.

**Beat 1: Recognition** — The customer notices the object, and it *speaks* before use.  
**Beat 2: Surrender** — They enter the ritual; time shifts.  
**Beat 3: Arrival** — Transformation happens (usually silent, felt, not explained).

**Example Script Structure**:
```
INT. KITCHEN - MORNING
[Hand reaches kettle—recognition moment]
[Water pours, steam rises—surrender moment]
NARRATOR: "Three minutes. Just... mine."
[Sip. Kitchen unchanged. Everything changed—arrival moment]
```

### Technique 2: Absurdist Staging
Use unexpected framings or juxtapositions that disrupt norm.

**Instead of**: Attractive person making tea (cliché).  
**Try**: Hands in close-up, kettle filmed like a sculpture, steam as a character, silence as the sound design.

Absurdism breaks past ad blindness by being genuinely *weird* in an honest way.

### Technique 3: Metaphorical Language (Showing, Not Telling)
The script *speaks* in images. No feature explanations.

**Not**: "This kettle boils water in 2 minutes."  
**Instead**: "The kettle sits still—almost sculptural. They lift it. The weight registers. Not a tool. An object. A choice."

The script *shows* the pause, doesn't narrate it.

### Technique 4: Narrative Subversion (Flipping Expectations)
Subvert the formula. Do the opposite of what ads typically do.

**Normal product ad**: Show the product, show benefit, show happy face.  
**Subversive ad**: Show the human, show the feeling, barely show the product. Let the object be a supporting actor in a human story.

## The Four-Step Creative Flow in TextFX

```
BRIEF (User input)
  ↓
[Constraint Reversal + Metaphor Mining]
  ↓
INSIGHT (What the customer secretly needs)
  ↓
[Cognitive Shift + Emotional Paradox]
  ↓
CONCEPT (New category positioning + metaphor strategy)
  ↓
[Emotional Beats + Narrative Subversion]
  ↓
SCRIPT (3-beat emotional story + absurdist staging)
```

Each step uses lateral thinking to break patterns and find human truth beneath surface needs.

## Practical Application for Copywriters

When using TextFX:

1. **Write a detailed brief** that includes:
   - Product/service
   - Target audience (demographics + emotional profile)
   - Market tension (what's the cultural conflict?)
   - Desired positioning

2. **Generate an insight** — TextFX will reverse constraints, find metaphors, and show hidden tensions.

3. **Review the insight** and note the tensions and reversals. These are your creative *material*.

4. **Map to concept** — TextFX reframes the category and builds a metaphor. Ask: "Do I believe this?"

5. **Write the script** — TextFX structures 3 emotional beats. You refine, add specificity, adjust for brand voice or media format.

6. **Iterate** — Adjust the brief (change the target tension or metaphor) and re-run. Each iteration reveals new angles.

## Prompt Templates (For AI Provider Integration)

### Insight Generator Prompt
```
You are a Creative Director practicing lateral thinking.

Given this brief: {brief}

1. Reverse the main constraint: Instead of solving the stated problem, invert it. What if the problem IS the solution?
2. Find unexpected connections: Link this product to 3 unrelated domains (luxury, meditation, design, nature, etc.). What do they reveal?
3. Mine metaphors: Complete: "This product is secretly a ___" (choose 3 poetic completions)
4. Map tensions: What contradictions exist between what the customer SAYS they want and what they FEEL they need?

Output a JSON with:
- insight: A 2-sentence synthesis
- tensions: Array of 3 human contradictions
- reversals: Array of 3 constraint inversions
- metaphors: Array of 3 poetic framings
- evidence: Array of cultural observations
```

### Concept Mapper Prompt
```
You are a Creative Director building an ad concept.

Given this insight: {insight}

1. Cognitive shift: Move the product out of its standard category into a new, unexpected one.
2. Emotional subversion: What does the customer secretly want vs. what they say?
3. Metaphor strategy: Choose ONE central metaphor that will guide every creative decision.
4. Emotional arc: Map a 3-beat journey (Before → During → After).

Output a JSON with:
- title: Concept name
- tagline: Single, surprising positioning line
- coreIdea: 2-3 sentences describing the new category + metaphor
- visualNotes: How should this feel and look?
- creativeDevice: The central metaphor
- emotionalArc: Before → During → After
- targetParadox: The core tension you're honoring
```

### Script Writer Prompt
```
You are a screenwriter crafting an emotional micro-drama.

Given this concept: {concept}

Write a 30-second script that:
1. Recognition beat (object speaks first)
2. Surrender beat (customer enters the ritual, time shifts)
3. Arrival beat (silent transformation)

Use metaphorical language. Show, don't tell. Barely mention the product—it's a supporting actor in a human story.

Output:
- script: The screenplay (format: INT. LOCATION - TIME, then action lines, beats, dialogue)
- beats: Array of 3 emotional beats with explanations
- cameraLanguage: How should this be filmed? (tone, movement, light)
- narrativeStrategy: What subversion are you using?
- emotionalTurning: The emotional arc for the viewer
```
